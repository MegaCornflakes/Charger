Post-Conventional: Stage Six

Stage Six is where people have made their own set of moral principles. They use these principles to make decisions, no matter what the consequences may be. Not many people make it to this stage. Example: Someone needs food to eat but in order to get it they have to break a law. They think it is morally wrong to not give a hungry person food so they steal it.
